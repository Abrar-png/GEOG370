In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

First, I downloaded the Indicatrix Mapper plugin into QGIS. This plugin allows Tissot's indicatrix to be calculated based on the given projection. After this, I imported the vector map into QGIS, and then on the bottom right corner of the window, there is an indicator that tells the user what the current projection is. Upon clicking this, I was able to change the projection. The plugin will also automatically recalculate Tissot's indicatrix.

WGS84 Projection

This projection attempts to keep the map as equidistant as possible. However, in preserving the distance, this projection sacrifices shape and size. This is evident as the projection moves toward the poles, the shape stretches horizontally and becomes enlarged.

Aitoff Projection

This projection shows the curvature of the Earth, and attempts to curve the projection in 2D. This causes the projection to be accurate at the Equator and the Prime Meridian but sacrifices the shape at the right and left sides of the map.

Pseudo Mercator

This projection aims to preserve the shapes of the countries but causes the size and distance to become distorted the further you get from the Equator.

Sphere Winkel

This projection portrays the Earth's curvature and tries to conserve distance. However, the projection stretches the map vertically and this stretch is more noticeable the closer you get to the poles.

Cylindrical Equal Area

This projection portrays the globe as the surface of a cylinder with equal diameter and height as the sphere. This conserves the size of countries for the most part, but as you move towards the poles, the countries get stretched horizontally.

World Equidistant Conic

This projection portrays the globe as the surface of a cone with equal base diameter and height as the sphere. This causes the point of the cone to be more accurate and portrays the world as being viewed from the top. However, as the projection moves away from the center(south), the shapes get stretched horizontally and the sizes get larger.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant

This projection is similar to the World Equidistant Conic projection. This projection attempts to keep verticle distances as equidistant as possible as the projection moves from north to south. Despite this, the map still becomes distorted in size and shape as the projection moves towards the edge.
